# Project Name: Wall-Climbing and Cleaning Robot (for Cobwebs) - v0.1

Team: Joseph Bolujo and David Arinde

## Aim:

To design and implement a wall-climbing robot using vacuum-based adhesion capable of cleaning cobwebs from vertical painted concrete surfaces with an accuracy >= 80%.

## Objectives:

- To design a robot capable of (i) climbing walls and (ii) cleaning walls
- To simulate a robot capable of i and ii above
- To implement a robot capable of i and ii above
- To evaluate the robot based on assigned metrics,

## Environment Consideration:

Wall Material: Painted Concrete
Surface Roughness: ?
Surface Inclination: Vertical

## Target Users:

Residential and Institutional buildings e.g. Auditoriums, Halls, Classes.
Current Case Study: Emlt04 and Sapetro, Faculty of Engineering, Redeemer's University.

## Methodology

### Bot Adhesion Method: Vacuum

This basically entails creating a vacuum between the bot and the surface of the wall to enable adhesion and facilitate climbing. This will be done using a brushless motor and an impeller.

**Note:** More on the methodology will be added after research has progressed.
